Vectors Vs Rasters

The images you will be working with can easily be divided into two categories, Vectors or Rasterized.

Raster (or bitmap) images are generally what you think about when thinking of images. These are the types of images that are produced when scanning or photographing an object.

Vector images, or line art, keep track of points and the equations for the lines that connect them. Vectors are made up of paths that are infinitely scalable because they work based on algorithms

Now let's make some T Shirts!

T Shirt Mockups

Open GIMP

Right click on this image and Download this picture.

The image is a PNG file with a transparent background, so it should open in GIMP with a checkerboard background.

Next, we’re going to import our logo as a new layer and set the blend mode to Darken Only. This will make it appear as if the logo is printed onto the shirt, with crease lines and shadows included. Choose your own logo from the previous logo making lesson, or use mine and place it on the shirt layer.

Make sure that the design you import is a PNG file with a transparent background.

That was pretty simple, but what about changing the color of the shirt?

Create a new layer above the shirt layer, set the mode to Multiply, then fill the layer in with whatever color you’d like the shirt to be.

But wait...

Our logo has changed as well. How do we fix that?

To fix this, we just have to delete a segment of the color layer so that the logo and can show through as it’s supposed to. Right click on the layer with the logo on it and select Alpha to Selection. This is going to create a selection around your logo.

Now click on the color layer and simply press Delete on your keyboard. As a result, the logo will now show through the deleted area.

And with that, we are done! We’ve created our t shirt mockup, add it to your Behance Portfolio.
